    • Who is Levi Celerio?

    Levi Celerio is a prolific Filipino composer and lyricist, born on April 30, 1910, in Tondo, Manila, Philippines. His parents, Cornelio Cruz and Juliana Celerio, were from Baliuag in Bulacan.The young Celerio inherited his love of music from his mother, a mananahi (seamstress) who played the harp and was a member of the church choir. He received a free education at the Academy of Music in Manila and became the youngest affiliate of the Manila Symphony Orchestra. Famous for being a lyricist, his songs treasure life; express nationalistic sentiments, and complete grand philosophies. He was awarded as National Artist of the Philippines for Music in 1997. He died on April 2, 2002.

    Interesting Facts about Celerio

    • Levi Celerio is probably best recognized as a leaf player, an achievement where he was placed in the Guinness Book of World Records.
    • He was a creative songwriter, with more than 4,000 songs to his acclaim including Filipino folk, Christmas, and love songs that are popular pieces, which many consider to be immortal.
    • He could not play instruments for long because he injured his arm while climbing a tree. In the meantime, Celerio temporarily worked as a comic illustrator.
    • Aside from being a musician, Celerio was also a poet and a film actor who appeared in various Philippine films of the 1950s and 1960s
    • Some of his well-known commercial songs are: "Saan Ka Man Naroroon?" "Kahit Konting Pagtingin," "Gaano Ko Ikaw Kamahal"
    • He is also known for the lyrics of folk melodies, like "Ang Pipit," "Tinikling," "Tunay Na Tunay," "Itik-Itik," "Waray-Waray,""Ako Ay May Singsing," "Pitong Gatang"

    Ang Pipit

    "Ang Pipit" (The Pipit) is a beloved Filipino folk song that narrates the tale of a bird recognized in Tagalog as a pipit, belonging to the Motacillidae family. This song unfolds the sad reality lived by Filipinos who remain captured in their own home country. Interestingly, some Filipinos may not be aware that "pipit" is also an English term. Consequently, there's a tendency to translate the title into English as "My Sparrow."

    Cover Versions / Interpretations

    Given the poignant and melancholic theme of this folk song, there exists a certain incongruity with contemporary sensibilities when one observes that the prevailing interpretations of the melody are decidedly upbeat. It's akin to joyfully mocking a vulnerable being caught in the depths of agony.
